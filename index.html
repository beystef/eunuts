The Causal Impact of EU Aflatoxin Regulations on Global Nut Trade Pricing

This project studies whether European Union aflatoxin regulations changed the pricing structure of bilateral exports in high-risk nuts and oilseeds. The core empirical question is whether stricter SPS enforcement pushed exporters toward quality upgrading, trade destruction, or both.

Read Proposal View EDA

Research Question

What is the causal impact of staggered EU aflatoxin regulations on bilateral export unit values for susceptible tree nuts and oilseeds?

Current Scope

Monthly bilateral export data at the HS6 level, 2000 to 2023, with current emphasis on cleaning, coverage checks, and descriptive patterns before regression-ready panel construction.

Project Roadmap

  1. Collect monthly bilateral export data from UN Comtrade for the target HS6 products.
  2. Filter structural noise and trim unit-value outliers using log-MAD rules.
  3. Build descriptive summaries for exporter coverage, importer coverage, and trade volumes.
  4. Construct a regression-ready panel with policy timing and tariff variables.
  5. Estimate baseline and event-study specifications.
